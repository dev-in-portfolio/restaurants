Housed in History

A look into the historic 1897 merchant roots and preserved tavern charm of Uptown's Fourth Ward.

The 1897 Storehouse

Our brick building at the corner of West 9th and Pine Streets was originally constructed in 1897. In its early days, it operated as Crowell's Grocery, serving the residents of Charlotte's Fourth Ward during the heights of the city's cotton mill era. Later, it became a local community pharmacy, acting as a central meeting point for neighbors.

As Charlotte grew and modern skyscrapers rose just blocks away, this building stood as a monument to early merchant history and community preservation.

Established in 1983

In 1983, the building was transformed into Alexander Michael's tavern. Great care was taken to preserve the architectural integrity of the historic structure. The original heart-of-pine floors, exposed brickwork, and antique wooden apothecary cabinets were meticulously restored to create the warm, timeless atmosphere you see today.

The original stained-glass window accents and brass light fixtures still catch the afternoon sun, maintaining a bridge to Charlotte's early 20th-century roots.

The Neighborhood Standard

At Al Mike's, we believe in a simple philosophy: good food, cold drinks, and honest conversations. We have resisted the push toward commercial corporate restaurant modernization. We don't take reservations, we don't host franchise promotions—we simply open our doors daily to serve our neighbors and travelers with the same warm hospitality that has defined us for over forty years.
