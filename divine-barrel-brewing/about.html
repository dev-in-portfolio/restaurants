Our Story

From a backyard brew rig to NoDa's destination taproom

Divine Barrel Brewing was born from a simple belief: great beer brings people together. Founders James Wilson and Mike Weber started experimenting with hop blends and barrel-aging in a shared garage, dreaming of a space where creativity and community could ferment side by side.

In 2020, that dream became reality when they opened the taproom on North Davidson Street in Charlotte's NoDa neighborhood. What started as a five-barrel system has grown into a 15-barrel production facility, but the spirit remains the same — bold flavors, experimental batches, and a warm welcome for everyone who walks through the door.

Today, Divine Barrel is known across the Southeast for our hazy IPAs, barrel-aged sours, and the kind of hospitality that makes strangers into regulars. We source local ingredients, collaborate with fellow Charlotte breweries, and pour every pint with pride.

2018
The Garage Days
James and Mike begin homebrewing on a 3-gallon system, experimenting with IPA hop combos and bourbon barrel stouts.
2019
Finding the Space
After a year of test batches and renter's insurance claims, the duo signs a lease at 3701 N Davidson St.
2020
Grand Opening
Divine Barrel opens its doors with 8 taps, a 5-bbl system, and a lineup built around Universal Language IPA.
2022
Expansion
Production scales to 15 bbl. Canning line installed. Distribution begins across Charlotte and surrounding counties.
2024
Barrel Program Matures
Dedicated barrel-aging room built. Snakes and Big Lubelski become cult favorites. Taproom hosts 100+ events per year.

Our Philosophy

What we stand for

Beer First

Every recipe starts with a question: "What if?" We push boundaries with hops, barrels, and fermentation, but never at the cost of drinkability.

Community Built

We host trivia, vinyl nights, run club, and fundraisers because a taproom should be more than a place to drink — it should be a gathering place.

Always Welcoming

Whether you're a beer nerd or trying your first IPA, you belong here. We're proud to be a pet-friendly, child-friendly, and LGBTQ+ welcoming space.

Locally Rooted

We partner with NC farms, roasters, and food purveyors. Our spent grain goes to local compost. We pour for our neighbors and sponsor neighborhood events.
