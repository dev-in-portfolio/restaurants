Pointed Toward Provenance

The story of HEX Coffee Roasters, from Plaza Midwood to Camp North End.

Bright by Design

HEX Coffee Roasters was founded in 2015 by John Michael Cord with a singular conviction: coffee should be unashamedly bright. In a landscape of dark roasts and obscuring blends, HEX chose the opposite path — light roasting that celebrates acidity, clarity, and the distinct taste of place.

"We don’t roast to a flavor profile. We roast to reveal the flavor that’s already there."

— John Michael Cord, Owner

The Philosophy

HEX is a terroir-first roastery. Every lot is sourced through direct relationships with producers who prioritize quality, sustainability, and transparency. The roasting approach is architecturally minded: subtract heat, add precision, and let the bean speak. The result is a cup that points back to where it was grown — not how it was processed in the drum.

Sourcing partners span four origin countries — Colombia, Kenya, Ethiopia, and Myanmar — each chosen for their potential to produce vibrant, clean, and memorable lots at light roast development.

Plaza Midwood Roots

The original HEX cafe opened at 1616 Central Ave in Charlotte’s historic Plaza Midwood neighborhood. A minimalist space with a custom bar, poured concrete counters, and a focused menu, it quickly became a destination for drinkers seeking something beyond the conventional third wave.

The cafe operates as a tasting room for the roastery — every drink is made with the same single-origin beans sold in bag, so customers can taste before they buy.

Camp North End Expansion

In 2022, HEX expanded to Camp North End — Charlotte’s sprawling creative campus — with HEX Coffee, Kitchen & Natural Wines. This second location brings together all three of the company’s passions: exceptional coffee, seasonal food, and low-intervention natural wines.
