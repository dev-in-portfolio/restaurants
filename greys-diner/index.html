Welcome to Grey's

Chef Sam Diminich — third-generation restaurateur, James Beard nominee — built Grey's Diner and Community Kitchen in the neighborhood that first felt like home. Plaza Midwood is where Sam found sobriety, community, and purpose. This diner is named after his son, Allan Grey.

In a 1948 Fodero Dining Car salvaged from New Jersey and hauled to Charlotte in 2005, Grey's brings fine-dining standards to the classic American diner. Every dish is sourced from local farms: Shipley Farms beef, Harmony Ridge eggs, Belly Brothers bacon, Enderly Coffee, Dukes bread, and Trey's Bagels.

No reservations. No pretension. Just a finer kind of diner.

What Makes Grey's Different

★

1948 Fodero Dining Car

Built in Newark, New Jersey. Survived Hurricane Floyd in 1999. Hauled to Charlotte in 2005 by Matt King. Original jukeboxes serve as booth accents. Every seat tells a story.

☘

Farm-to-Table

Sourced from the best local producers: Shipley Farms beef, Harmony Ridge Farm eggs, Belly Brothers bacon, Enderly Coffee roasters, Dukes bread, and Trey's Bagels. Every plate connects to a partner.

♥

Community Kitchen

No reservations needed. Walk in, grab a seat, and become part of the neighborhood. This is a diner for the community, rooted in Plaza Midwood.
