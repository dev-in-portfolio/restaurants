Our Story

A diner, a car, and a community.

Chef Sam Diminich

Third-generation restaurateur. James Beard nominee. A chef who built his career in fine dining and decided to bring those standards to the most American of institutions: the diner.

Sam's path to Grey's wasn't a straight line. Plaza Midwood was his first Charlotte home — the neighborhood where he found sobriety, purpose, and community. He knows the people here. He knows the streets. This isn't a chef opening a concept in an unfamiliar neighborhood. This is home.

Grey's is named after his son, Allan Grey.

The 1948 Fodero Dining Car

Built in 1948 in Newark, New Jersey. A Fodero Dining Car — one of the last of its kind, built in an era when diner cars were the gathering places of American life.

This car survived Hurricane Floyd in 1999. It endured floods, storms, and decades of wear before being salvaged and hauled to Charlotte in 2005 by Matt King. For years, it lived its second life as Mattie's Diner — a beloved neighborhood spot.

Now it's Grey's. The original jukeboxes remain as booth accents. The counter still curves. The car's patina tells its own story — one of survival, reinvention, and community. When you sit in the Fodero car, you're sitting in living history.

A Finer Kind of Diner

The idea is simple: take the warmth, the accessibility, the soul of a classic American diner, and elevate it with the sourcing, the technique, and the care of fine dining. Every ingredient is traceable. Every dish is made from scratch. Every plate reflects Chef Sam's standards.

Grey's Diner and Community Kitchen is part of the Your Farms Your Table (YFYT) family — alongside Restaurant Constance, Sam's Michelin-recognized restaurant. YFYT is the thread that connects all of Sam's work: local farms, direct relationships, honest food.
