Peruvian Culinary Heritage

Matthews and Pineville's favorite Peruvian kitchen, preserving multi-generational culinary arts since 2012.

Founded in Pineville 2012

Machu Picchu Peruvian Cuisine & Bar was founded in 2012 in downtown Pineville. Our mission has always been simple: to present the authentic, diverse, and vibrant flavors of Peru to North Carolina. From our fresh coastal key lime cures to slow-cooked mountain beef stews, we preserve traditional recipes passed down through generations.

Whether celebrating over a family Lomo Saltado or grabbing fresh empanadas after work, our restaurant serves as a warm Peruvian community hub.

The Chifa Fusion Legacy

Peruvian food is one of the world's original culinary fusion cuisines. In the late 19th century, Chinese immigrants arriving in Peru integrated Cantonese wok-cooking techniques with native Andean ingredients, giving birth to "Chifa" cuisine.

This fusion created Lomo Saltado—where tender beef strips are sizzled over high flame with red onions, tomatoes, and soy-garlic sauce, served over thick potato fries.

Andean Ingredients & Superfoods

We import authentic Peru-grown ingredients to ensure traditional flavors. Our pantry features giant white-kernel Choclo corn, sweet Camote potatoes, aromatic Ají Amarillo yellow peppers, purple corn (Maíz Morado) for brewing fresh Chicha Morada juices, and fiery Ají Limo red chilies for curing raw fish to perfection.
