Steaming Heritage

The story of Chef Larry Schreiber bringing authentic Asian street-food comfort to Charlotte's historic mill.

Chef Larry Schreiber's Vision

Bao and Broth is the result of Chef Larry Schreiber’s passion for street foods. He envisioned a fast-casual dining stall where busy Charlotte locals could enjoy premium, scratch-made Asian street comfort food without sacrificing quality.

Every bowl of ramen and steamed bao bun reflects a balance of traditional Asian broth-simmering techniques and modern culinary precision.

Sourcing & 24-Hour Broths

Our signature Tonkotsu broth requires patient preparation. We slow-cook pork bones in filtered water for a full 24 hours, allowing the marrow and collage to dissolve completely, creating our creamy, full-bodied signature soup base.

Our noodles are sourced fresh from premium ramen makers, providing a firm, wavy bite that holds our rich broths beautifully.

Our Historic Food Stall

Located inside Optimist Hall, Bao and Broth sits within a beautifully restored 1890s textile mill. Originally operating as the Highland Park Manufacturing Company Mill No. 1, the historic brick and high beam structure now serves as Charlotte's premier dining destination, providing the perfect energetic backdrop for our busy steam counters.
