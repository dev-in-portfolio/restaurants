Our Story

Big Texas flavor in the heart of the Carolinas — built on craft, community, and 230+ reasons to stay awhile.

The Deliciousness Is in the Details

When Frank Scibelli and Dennis Thompson set out to bring authentic Tex-Mex to Charlotte, they didn't chase trends — they chased truth. They recruited Rob Walsh, James Beard award-winning culinary historian, Texas food authority, and author of The Tex-Mex Cookbook, to anchor the kitchen in genuine tradition.

Rob's philosophy is simple: Tex-Mex isn't "Mexican food gone wrong" — it's a distinct regional cuisine born on the border, shaped by Mexican home cooking, Texas ranch culture, and the ingredients at hand. Wood-fired grills. Handmade tortillas. Chili con carne that simmers for hours. Cheese enchiladas smothered in a gravy that remembers every hand that stirred it.

At Paco's, that philosophy hits the plate every day. Our fajitas kiss a real mesquite grill. Our tortillas are pressed fresh each morning. Our salsas are made in small batches — no buckets, no shortcuts. And behind the bar, over 230 tequila expressions tell the story of agave from Jalisco's highlands to the valleys of Oaxaca.

The mesquite grill — where every fajita begins

Three Pillars

Authenticity Over Aesthetics

We don't plate for Instagram — we cook for flavor. Every recipe traces back to a specific Texas border town, a family kitchen, or a taqueria that's fed generations. If it's not on a menu in San Antonio, Laredo, or El Paso, it's not on ours.

The Agave Library

230+ tequilas isn't a number — it's a curriculum. Our bar team studies terroir, production methods, and aging nuances so they can guide you from a crisp Blanco to a 12-year Extra Añejo like a sommelier guides wine.

Hospitalidad

In Texas, the table is where life happens. We build our spaces — from the patio to La Caja — for lingering. Share a pitcher. Order another round. Stay for dessert. The check can wait.

From Concept to Community

2021

SouthPark Opens

First location debuts at 6401 Carnegie Blvd in the Specialty Shops at SouthPark. The 5,200 sq ft space features a 40-seat bar, covered patio, and two private dining rooms.

2022

Tequila Program Recognized

Named "Best Tequila Selection in Charlotte" by Charlotte Magazine. Bar team begins quarterly agave education series for guests.

2023

Fort Mill Expansion

Second location opens at 1328 Broadcloth St in Fort Mill, SC — bringing Tex-Mex across the border with a private lot and expanded outdoor dining.

2024

Private Dining Growth

La Caja and Main Dining Room host 300+ events annually — corporate dinners, rehearsal dinners, birthday fiestas, and holiday parties.

2025

Catering & Fiesta Menus Launch

Full off-site catering program debuts with build-your-own taco bars, fajita stations, and family-style enchilada trays for 20–500 guests.

Part of the FS Food Group Family

Paco's Tacos & Tequila is a proud concept of FS Food Group, the Charlotte-based hospitality collective founded by Frank Scibelli. For over two decades, FS Food Group has built restaurants that become neighborhood institutions — each with its own soul, its own regulars, its own story.

When you dine at Paco's, you're experiencing the same commitment to craft that defines every FS Food Group kitchen: scratch-made everything, locally-sourced when possible, and a fierce loyalty to the communities we serve.

What We Stand For

Scratch-Made Daily

Tortillas pressed at 6 AM. Salsas blended in batches. Chili simmered 6 hours. No shortcuts, no Sysco bags, no "just add water."

Agave Education

Free quarterly tequila classes. Flight cards with tasting notes. Staff certified by Tequila Regulatory Council. We want you to learn, not just drink.

Community First

FS Food Group donates 1% of revenue to local food banks. Paco's hosts quarterly "Tacos for Teachers" and "First Responders Friday" events.

Transparency

We'll tell you exactly which ranch the beef came from, which farm the corn, which distillery the tequila. If we don't know, we'll find out.
