    A smoother group night

    Three things worth deciding early.

    Good group dinners do not need a rigid itinerary. They just need one person to handle the useful details before everyone arrives.

    01 · ARRIVAL

    Pick a window

    Give everyone a fifteen-minute arrival range instead of one exact minute. It keeps the first round calmer.

    02 · TABLE

    Know the count

    A reliable headcount helps the restaurant understand the seating need and keeps the group from splitting unexpectedly.

    03 · POLICIES

    Ask before bringing extras

    Birthday cakes, decorations, separate checks and large-group seating should be confirmed before the visit.
