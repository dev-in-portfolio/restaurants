About Amina

Heritage. Fire. Legacy.

From Charlotte’s Kitchen to Charlotte’s Table

Chef Chianti Peek is a Charlotte native who earned the title “food surgeon” for a reason: precision, artistry, and an obsessive attention to detail. Before opening Amina, Chef Chianti served as a private chef to some of the biggest names in professional sports — NBA star Kemba Walker and NFL icon Cam Newton among them.

But the food Chianti grew up on — the berbere spice, the sambusa folded by hand, the tibs sizzling in the pan — that was always the foundation. Amina is the expression of those roots, elevated.

A New Vision for Charlotte

Opening on April 3, 2026, Amina was immediately recognized as one of the Charlotte Observer’s biggest restaurant openings of the year. The concept is singular: Eritrean-Mediterranean fine dining by night, late-night lounge by midnight.

The space — approximately 5,350 square feet — features a striking 40-foot bar, seating for roughly 170 guests, and a design language that balances warm cream and gold with deep black and burgundy. It’s fine dining that doesn’t take itself too seriously, and a lounge that takes the night very seriously indeed.

What We Stand For

Heritage First

Eritrean flavors are not a trend for us — they are the backbone. Every berbere rub, every awaze marinade, every sambusa is made with intention and respect for tradition.

Fire as Method

Our wood-fired program isn’t decoration. It’s how we build flavor — from branzino to flatbreads, the fire is part of every dish.

Night as Experience

The late-night lounge is designed for people who want more than dinner. It’s a space to stay, to celebrate, to feel the city.
