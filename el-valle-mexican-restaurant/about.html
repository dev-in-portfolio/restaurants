Our Story

About El Valle

A family dream rooted in the traditions of a small Mexican village.

Our Name, Our Roots

El Valle — meaning "The Valley" — is named after the village in Jalisco, Mexico, where our founder's family roots run deep. It's a tribute to the land, the people, and the culinary traditions that shaped who we are.

Founded in 1998

What started as a small restaurant in Matthews, North Carolina, was born from a simple idea: share authentic, homestyle Mexican food with a community far from home. Our founder brought family recipes across the border — dishes passed down for generations — and opened the doors of El Valle for the very first time.

The Charlotte area welcomed us with open arms, and word quickly spread about our food. People came for the cheese dip and stayed for the warmth of a family that treated every guest like one of their own.

Growing With Our Community

From that original Matthews location, El Valle has grown to three restaurants across the Charlotte area, plus our taco truck that brings the flavors of El Valle directly to your neighborhood. But no matter how much we've grown, the heart of our restaurant has never changed.

Every dish is still prepared with the same love and care as it was in 1998. Our ingredients are fresh. Our recipes are authentic. And our commitment to treating every guest like family is as strong as ever.

More Than a Restaurant

El Valle is a gathering place. It's where families celebrate birthdays over sizzling fajitas, where friends share chips and queso on Friday nights, and where our team comes together every day to serve the community we love. We are proud to be part of the Charlotte area, and we look forward to serving you for many more years to come.
