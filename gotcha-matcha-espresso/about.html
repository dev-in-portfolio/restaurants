🐼 Our Story

About

A Starbucks barista, a trip to Thailand, and a panda named Gotcha.

The Beginning

Columbia, SC — The Barista

Eric "Braek Haven" Hicklen Jr. grew up in Columbia, South Carolina, where he worked as a barista at Starbucks. He loved the craft — the ritual of pulling shots, steaming milk, and connecting with customers over coffee. But something was missing. He knew he wanted to build something of his own.

2019

The Thailand Trip That Changed Everything

In 2019, Eric took a trip to Thailand. Walking through a bustling market, he tried matcha served in a Belgian waffle cone — a perfect fusion of flavors and cultures. It was a revelation. He started researching matcha obsessively, learning about ceremonial grade quality, Thai flavor profiles like pandan and ube, and the culture behind the drink.

That trip planted a seed. He couldn't stop thinking about bringing that experience back to the Carolinas.

Sept 2023

The Pop-Up Era

Eric launched Gotcha Matcha as a pop-up at The Coterie Concept, a wine bar in Charlotte's South End neighborhood. The concept was simple: ceremonial grade matcha with Thai-inspired flavors, served with personality. The panda branding — playful, memorable, unmistakable — became an instant hit.

People lined up. The matcha was smooth, never gritty. The pandan and ube flavors were unlike anything else in Charlotte. Word spread fast.

2024

From Pop-Up to Permanent

The pop-up proved the demand was real. Eric moved Gotcha Matcha to Tabbris, a co-working space, continuing to refine the menu and build a loyal following. Then in November 2024, he opened a permanent location in Uptown Charlotte — inside an office building at 200 S Tryon St, Suite S112.

The Uptown spot was a game-changer. A real home for the brand. Office workers, students, matcha enthusiasts — they all found the panda.

March 2026

Top Matcha in Charlotte

By early 2026, Gotcha Matcha had earned a 4.8 rating on Google (188 reviews) and a 4.9 on Yelp (55 reviews). Yelp and the Charlotte Observer named it the top matcha spot in Charlotte.

That same month, Eric expanded — opening a second Uptown location to meet the demand. Two Uptown spots, one vision: bring the best matcha experience to Charlotte.

Now

The Panda Bar

Today, Gotcha Matcha & Espresso is a Black-owned Uptown Charlotte staple. Staff wear panda heads. The menu features 13+ matcha drinks, 8 espresso options, and Thai-inspired pastries like ube and pandan donuts and cinnamon rolls. Every drink is customizable — sweetness and ice levels, your way.

It's more than a matcha bar. It's the proof that a barista with a vision, a trip to Thailand, and a whole lot of heart can build something truly special.
