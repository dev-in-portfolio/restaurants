Our Story

How a vision for an authentic Parisian brasserie found its home in the heart of Uptown Charlotte.

Bank of America Corporate Center — 300 S Tryon St

The Vision

La Belle Hélène was conceived as a love letter to the great brasseries of Paris — those bustling, luminous spaces where the energy never falters and the food is at once timeless and alive.

When the 5th Street Group identified the 6,800-square-foot corner space in the Bank of America Corporate Center, they saw an opportunity to bring that spirit to Charlotte. The result is a dining room that captures the romance of a Parisian passageway: 25-ft coffered ceilings, hand-blown Italian chandeliers, a sweeping pewter bar top, and operable windows that dissolve the boundary between indoors and the Tryon Street sidewalk.

"We wanted to create a place that felt both grand and intimate," says the team. "A brasserie where you could celebrate an anniversary on a Tuesday or close a deal over lunch — and feel equally at home either way."

The Chef

Chef Jamie Lynch is one of Charlotte's most recognized culinary talents. A Bravo Top Chef alum (Season 11 and 14), Jamie has built a career defined by technical precision, deep respect for French technique, and a Southern cook's instinct for seasonal ingredients.

As Chef Partner at 5th Street Group, Jamie oversees the culinary vision for La Belle Hélène. His menu walks the line between reverence and reinvention — boeuf bourguignon that tastes like Lyon, but with Carolina-raised beef; sole meunière that honors the classics, with vegetables from nearby farms.

"French food is the foundation of modern fine dining," Jamie says. "But a brasserie shouldn't be precious. It should be convivial, generous, and full of life. That's what we're building here."

Chef Jamie Lynch — Bravo Top Chef Alum
5th Street Group

The Group

5th Street Group is a Charlotte-based hospitality company known for creating distinctive dining destinations that reflect the character of their neighborhoods. Their portfolio includes some of the city's most celebrated restaurants, each with a unique identity and a shared commitment to exceptional service.

La Belle Hélène represents the group's most ambitious project to date — a 166-seat French brasserie with four private dining rooms, a 30-seat patio, and a design that transports guests from Tryon Street to the 6th arrondissement in a single step.

"Charlotte has become a world-class dining city," the group notes. "We felt it was time for a world-class brasserie."
