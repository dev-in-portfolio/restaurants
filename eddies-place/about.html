📖 Our Story

About Eddie's Place

A New Orleans dream that became a Charlotte institution.

A Trip to New Orleans

In the mid-1990s, Eddie Gallant took a trip to New Orleans. He fell in love with the food, the music, and the way a restaurant could feel like the center of a neighborhood. When he came back to Charlotte, he knew he wanted to build something with that same spirit.

In 1997, Eddie's Place opened on Sharon Amity Road in Cotswold. The menu blended Cajun flavors with classic American comfort food. The atmosphere was warm, the portions were generous, and the prices were fair. It didn't take long for the neighborhood to adopt it as their own.

A Neighborhood Gathering Place

Today, Eddie's Place is where Charlotte's movers and shakers gather alongside families, regulars, and first-timers. Judges, CEOs, working men and women—everyone sits at the same counter. Most of our staff has been here for five years or more. Servers remember your name after a couple of visits.

We've been named one of Southern Living's Best Restaurants in Charlotte, ranked among the city's best breakfast spots, and featured in the Charlotte Observer. But the thing we're most proud of is simpler than any award: people come back. Again and again.
