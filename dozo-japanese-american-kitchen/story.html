Our Story

From a food truck to Charlotte's favorite intimate katsu counter

DŌZO Japanese-American Kitchen started as a food truck — Katsu Kart Sando Shop — serving crispy katsu sandwiches at breweries and pop-ups across Charlotte. The response was immediate: lines formed, sandwiches sold out, and it became clear that Charlotte was hungry for this kind of bold, unfussy Japanese comfort food.

In 2022, the team opened their first brick-and-mortar on Thrift Road — an intimate 12-seat space with a 6-seat chef's counter where guests could watch every cutlet get panko-crusted and fried to order. The second location followed on East Boulevard in Dilworth, bringing the same menu to a new neighborhood.

The name DŌZO means "please" or "go ahead" in Japanese — an invitation to eat well, share freely, and enjoy the moment. That spirit guides everything we do, from the curry we simmer for hours to the sandos we build by hand.
