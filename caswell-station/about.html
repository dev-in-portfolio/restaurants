📚 Our Story

About Caswell Station

The big house on the corner of Pecan and Caswell

Caswell Station started with a house. A big, beautiful, Elizabeth-neighborhood house on the corner of Pecan and Caswell. Rather than let it become just another Charlotte development story, someone saw what it could be: a place where the neighborhood could gather, eat, drink, and watch the game.

The Neighborhood

Elizabeth is one of Charlotte's oldest neighborhoods, and Caswell Station is built into its fabric. We're the place you walk to on a Saturday morning for brunch. The spot you hit after work on a Friday. The corner where you run into your neighbors and end up staying for three more drinks.

The Food

Our menu is New American — creative, sharable, and unpretentious. Tatchos (tater tots with short ribs and gravy), cheesesteak egg rolls, wings in four sauces, tacos with everything from Baja fish to pork belly. We also do a weekend brunch that the neighborhood takes seriously.

The Vibe

Casual. Loud. Fun. We've got TVs for every game, a patio for warm nights, live music on select evenings, and a bar that keeps things moving. We're not trying to be anything we're not — we're your neighborhood pub, and we're good at it.
