Our Story

Where neighbors become friends, and friends become family

Fenian's Keep opened its doors in NoDa in March 2026 with a simple mission: to be the neighborhood's living room. No food. No pretension. Just great drinks, good company and a warm place to call your own at the end of the day.

Owner Kyle Musumeci, who also runs Brigid's Irish Pub in South End, brought the same community-first philosophy north to NoDa. "My biggest thing has always been being part of the neighborhood," Musumeci says. "Those are the places I like to go to. It's your neighborhood spot where everyone knows you and everyone knows everyone else's name — that is the community vibe that you want in a pub."

The name Fenian's Keep pays respect to the rich Irish pub tradition — a "keep" being a stronghold or gathering place, and "Fenian" rooted in the ancient Irish warrior bands, the Fianna. It's a name that evokes strength, community and the enduring spirit of Irish hospitality. Like the legends before it, Fenian's Keep stands as a place of refuge, warmth and belonging.

"There is no stranger at an Irish pub — only friends you haven't met yet."

— Fenian's Keep

From the start, Fenian's Keep has focused exclusively on what it does best: spirits and ales. The bar program features a carefully selected range of Irish whiskeys, from the familiar Jameson to single-pot-still treasures like Redbreast 12 Year and Green Spot. The taps pour Guinness the way it's meant to be served, alongside rotating local Charlotte craft brews and European imports. And for those looking for something mixed, the cocktail menu puts an Irish spin on the classics — the Irish Mule, the Fenian house signature and a proper Irish Coffee among them.

With TVs for sports, live music on select nights and a jukebox that sets the tone, Fenian's Keep is designed to be part of the rhythm of NoDa — one more stop on a night out, or the only stop you need. Every day brings $5 shot specials and a rotating slate of drink deals that keep things interesting.

Fenian's Keep is still writing its story. New drinks, new traditions and new friends arrive every day. But the belief at its heart stays the same: a great pub isn't about what's on the menu — it's about who's at the bar.
