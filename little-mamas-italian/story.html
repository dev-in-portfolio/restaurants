Our Story

Inspired by the old home-style Italian restaurants of New England from the 60s and 70s — really well done classics, without pretense.

Little Mama's was born from a love for the kind of Italian restaurant that's getting harder to find: a place where the pasta is made fresh daily, the mozzarella is pulled by hand, and the parmigiana comes out of the broiler bubbling and golden. The kind of place where a stiff cocktail is taken seriously, but the vibe is anything but stiff.

Always fresco! Really well done classics, without pretense. A stiff cocktail and a proper bowl of fresh pasta or a knob of just-made mozzarella are the simple but elegant meals that keep us in business. And the broiler menu will impress the whole table with its simplicity and deliciousness. Molto Buono! — The Little Mama's Philosophy

We take our inspiration from the Italian-American restaurants of New England in the 1960s and 70s — red sauce joints where families gathered, where the portions were generous, and where every meal felt like Sunday dinner. We don't try to reinvent the wheel. We just make really good Italian food, the way it should be made.

Our pasta is made fresh in-house using imported Italian flour and farm-fresh eggs. Our mozzarella is stretched and pulled daily. Our meatballs are a blend of Wagyu beef and pork. Our sauces simmer low and slow. And our broiler — well, that's where the magic happens.
