Our Story

From a dream of Paris to Charlotte's beloved French bakery

"We wanted to create a place where every detail feels like a happy discovery — like wandering into a Parisian attic full of treasures." — The Amélie's Team

How It Began

Amélie's French Bakery & Café opened its first doors in Charlotte's NoDa neighborhood with a simple belief: that great food and beautiful surroundings can transport you. Inspired by the sidewalk cafés, flea markets, and patisseries of Paris, the founders set out to build a space that felt both elegant and wonderfully eclectic.

What started as a single bakery grew through word of mouth — and through the love of Charlotte locals who craved authentic croissants, colorful macarons, and a place to linger. Today, Amélie's spans four locations across the city, each one filled with mismatched vintage chairs, local artwork, and the aroma of butter and coffee.

Our Journey

2005

The first Amélie's opens in NoDa — a tiny bakery with big dreams and a line out the door.

2010

A second location arrives on Park Road, bringing French pastries to south Charlotte.

2016

Carmel Commons opens, featuring an expanded kitchen and catering operation.

2019

The Uptown location debuts in the heart of Charlotte's financial district.

Today

Four locations, dozens of pastries, and one enduring commitment to quality and whimsy.

Community & Craft

Amélie's believes in supporting the community that supports us. We partner with local bakeries and farms, source ingredients thoughtfully, and donate day-old pastries to neighborhood organizations. Our cafés serve as gathering spaces for local meetups, art openings, and fundraisers.

Every pastry is made from scratch using traditional French techniques. Our bakers arrive before dawn to laminate dough, whip pastry cream, and bake baguettes. It is labor-intensive and absolutely worth it — because we believe that the best food comes from care, not shortcuts.
