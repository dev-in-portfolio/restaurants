Our Story

How a husband-and-wife team brought destination dining to a small college town.

JK

Joe Kindred

Chef & Owner

A Virginia native, Joe trained in some of the country's most celebrated kitchens before returning to the South. His cooking blends Italian technique with a deep reverence for Southern ingredients — a philosophy that earned Kindred a James Beard nomination and a spot on Bon Appetit's Hot Ten.

KK

Katy Kindred

Owner & Creative Director

Katy brings the vision to life — designing the dining experience, curating the mismatched plates and flatware, and shaping the warm, homespun atmosphere. Together with Joe, she has built a restaurant group that includes Hello, Sailor, Albertine, and milkbread cafe.

The Journey

Before Kindred

Training in Great Kitchens

Joe cooked at some of the country's most celebrated restaurants, honing a style that would later define Kindred: precise technique, seasonal ingredients, and an Italian soul with Southern roots.

2014

The Pharmacy on Main Street

Joe and Katy discovered a vacant former pharmacy at 131 N Main St in Davidson, NC. The space — with its crisp white tiles, exposed brick, and hardwood floors — felt like home. They could see it: the kitchen downstairs, the dining room upstairs, and a hidden staircase connecting them.

2016

Kindred Opens

Kindred opened its doors to immediate acclaim. Critics praised the Milk Bread, the Squid Ink Pasta, and the singular warmth of the space. Bon Appetit named it one of the Hot Ten Best New Restaurants in America.

2017 & Beyond

National Recognition

Kindred earned a James Beard Award nomination, a spot on The New York Times' Best Restaurant list, and a place in the MICHELIN Guide. The Kindred team expanded, opening Hello, Sailor on Lake Norman, Albertine in Davidson, and milkbread cafe — each a different expression of the same ethos.

The Space

Before it was a dining destination, 131 N Main St was a neighborhood pharmacy. Joe and Katy preserved the bones of the building — the white tile, the brick, the hardwood — and transformed it into a restaurant that feels both timeless and unmistakably new.

The two-story layout is part of the magic: the kitchen hums on the lower floor while guests dine upstairs, the muffled sounds of a busy service floating up through the stairwell.
