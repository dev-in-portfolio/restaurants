Our Story

A pasta bar born from the garden and the kitchen

From the Garden to the Open Kitchen

Since 2018, Flour Shop has been Charlotte's neighborhood pasta bar — a place where the kitchen is the stage and handmade pasta is the star. Tucked into the backlot of Park Road Shopping Center, Chef Trey Wilson built something intimate and honest: a 24-seat pasta bar where every dish is made from scratch, right in front of you.

The concept is simple but rare. Everything is made in-house — the pasta, the sauces, the bread, the desserts. Ingredients come from local farms and fishermen across the Carolinas. And a good portion comes from Chef Trey's own backyard garden, where he grows herbs and produce specifically for the restaurant.

This isn't farm-to-table as a trend. It's the way this kitchen has always worked.

The Pasta Bar

24 seats, open kitchen, one chef's vision. Sit at the bar and watch your meal come together — from rolling the dough to the final plate.

Everything In-House

Pasta made daily. Sauces from scratch. Bread baked fresh. If it can be made here, it is.

Chef Trey Wilson

Chef Trey's approach is rooted in respect for ingredients and craft. His backyard garden supplies the restaurant with seasonal herbs and produce. He sources from local farms and fishermen — ethical, sustainable, and as fresh as it gets. The result is a menu that changes with the seasons and always feels personal.

Under his direction, Flour Shop has earned recognition as one of Charlotte's best restaurants from Southern Living, and has been an OpenTable Diners' Choice winner multiple years running — currently holding a 4.7 rating from over 1,300 reviews.
