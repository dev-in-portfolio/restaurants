The Story of Polk Street

Serving hot coffee, griddle pancakes, and friendly conversation in Pineville since 1978.

Pineville's Morning Anchor

The Dive N opened in 1978 in historic downtown Pineville. Housed in a classic storefront on Polk Street, the diner was designed to replicate the classic retro diners of mid-century America. Featuring vinyl-topped counter stools, steel griddles, and checker details, it immediately became the primary meeting place for locals before starting their workdays.

While Pineville has expanded from a quiet textile mill town to a thriving Charlotte suburb, The Dive N has remained a preservation of classic Southern diner culture.

The Polk Street Counter

Our counter is where the community gathers. On any given morning, you will find city managers, local shop owners, high school coaches, and families sitting side-by-side.

We believe in honest, fast service and warm griddle meals. We don't have electronic ordering tablets or touch screens at the counter—our servers still write orders on paper tickets, and our cooks shout back when a fresh patty melt or Junkyard Plate is ready for the window.

Unapologetically Diner

We embrace the "Dive" in our name. To us, it stands for unpretentious quality, huge portions, hot coffee that never runs dry, and a kitchen that treats every diner like a regular. We don't cut corners on our food—our onion rings are hand-battered daily, our beef patties are pressed fresh, and our pimento cheese is made using a secret family recipe.
