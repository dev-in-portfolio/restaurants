Our Story

A Victorian house, a warm cup, and a community that feels like home.

Alice Jules Coffee House sits in a beautifully preserved Victorian home in the historic district of Monroe, North Carolina — a quiet alternative to the drive-thru and the chain.

The building itself sets the tone: hardwood floors, vintage trim, a porch that invites you to sit and stay. Inside, cognac-colored stools, cozy nooks, and rotating displays of local art make every visit feel personal. It’s the kind of place where the owner might remember your order.

Every day starts with three fresh roasts: Alice’s House Brew, a flavored roast, and a bold roast. The espresso drinks are hand-pulled. The pastries come from local bakeries. The syrups are flavored with care, not from a wholesale catalog.

“Where the coffee is brewed especially for YOU.”

Community First

Alice Jules is more than a coffee shop — it’s a community hub. Local artists display their work on the walls. Handmade jewelry and gifts from Monroe makers are sold near the counter. The shop hosts and supports local events, from holiday gatherings to fundraisers.

Regulars stop in for the Mr. Darcy (English toffee latte) and stay for the conversation. First-timers are greeted like returning friends. The loyalty program rewards visits, but the real draw is the atmosphere: warm, unhurried, and unmistakably local.

More Than a Cup

Alice Jules also sells its own coffee by the bag — whole bean or ground — so you can take the taste home. The retail selection includes local honey, handmade mugs, and gifts you won’t find at a chain.

Whether you stop in for a morning drip coffee, an afternoon blended smoothie, or a weekend chai latte with a pastry, the experience is the same: a small, independent coffeehouse that takes its coffee seriously and its customers personally.

Come sit a while. You’re welcome here.
