The Soul of Brevard Street

Fifty years of family, community, and the best burgers in the Queen City.

Founded in 1973

Brooks' Sandwich House was founded in 1973 by twins David and Floyd Brooks. Originally, the red wooden shack on North Brevard Street was built to serve cheap, fast, and filling meals to railway workers, industrial warehouse hands, and truck drivers who dominated the local neighborhood at the time.

The menu was kept minimal, focusing on walk-up window service where guests would take their food to stand at wooden high-top tables outdoors—a setup that remains identical to this day.

Anchor of the NoDa Neighborhood

Over the decades, the surrounding neighborhood transitioned from industrial mills to NoDa (North Davidson), Charlotte's premier arts district full of trendy bars, galleries, and apartments. While the skyline transformed, Brooks' Sandwich House remained unchanged.

We became a bridge between old and new Charlotte. Bankers, artists, construction crews, and tourists all queue together at our window, united by their craving for our chili-topped burgers and hot dogs.

Community & Resilience

In 2019, our family suffered the tragic loss of co-owner Scott Brooks. The outpouring of love and support from the Charlotte community was overwhelming. Thousands of locals lined Brevard Street, raising funds and offering support to ensure the windows at Brooks' would reopen. Today, we carry on Scott's legacy with pride, serving every burger and dog with the same dedication and local love that has defined us for over half a century.
