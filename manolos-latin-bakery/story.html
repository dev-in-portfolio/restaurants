Our Story

From Colombia to Charlotte — a journey of bread, community, and purpose.

Manolo's Latin Bakery began in 1997 with a simple vision: bring the warmth and flavor of authentic Latin bread to Charlotte. Founder Manolo Betancur, a Colombian-born engineer, former naval officer, and martial artist, traded his uniform for an apron and set out to build something that mattered.

What started as a small bakery on Central Avenue grew into Charlotte's first Latin bakery — a place where the aroma of fresh-baked conchas, the richness of tres leches cake, and the boldness of Colombian coffee come together under one roof.

For over 29 years, Manolo's has perfected the art of Latin baking. Every concha, every empanada, every slice of tres leches is made from scratch using treasured family recipes. The bakery now offers over 65 varieties of Latin bread and 11 unique tres leches flavors — from classic vanilla caramel to Cheerwine and Nutella.

But Manolo's is more than a bakery — it's a purpose-driven brand. As a World Bread Hero USA 2021 honoree, Manolo's supports 26+ local nonprofits, from Ukraine humanitarian aid to community food programs. The staff proudly wears shirts that read "Made in America by Immigrant Hands" — a testament to the bakery's belief in dignity, hard work, and giving back.

Today, Manolo's Bakery is a Charlotte institution — recognized by Stanford's State of Latino Entrepreneurship report, featured on PBS, MSNBC, and CNN, and beloved by a community that has walked through its doors for three decades.
