Our Story

More than two decades of serving Charlotte's University City community — a family tradition.

From Humble Beginnings

When the Leng family opened their doors in 2004 on W Mallard Creek Church Road, they had a simple belief: great food shouldn't cost a fortune. What started as a small takeout counter quickly grew into a beloved neighborhood institution, known for generous portions, bold Szechuan flavors, and a warm welcome for every guest.

Over the years, House of Leng has expanded to include Japanese classics — sushi rolls, hibachi grills, and teriyaki bowls — all prepared with the same commitment to quality and value. The family has remained hands-on in the kitchen every day, ensuring each dish meets the standards that kept their first customers coming back.

For the Leng family, this restaurant has never just been a business. It is where their children grew up, where neighbors became lifelong friends, and where the University City community has gathered for celebrations, weeknight dinners, and everything in between.

Our Journey

2004

The Leng family opens House of Leng on W Mallard Creek Church Road. A modest takeout menu featuring Chinese and Szechuan classics.

2008

Expanded the kitchen and added a small dining area. Regulars begin to fill the seats — word of mouth brings in the University crowd.

2012

Introduced sushi and hibachi to the menu. The family sent chefs to train in Japanese cuisine to ensure authentic preparation.

2016

Opened a second location to serve more of University City. Hired local staff and deepened ties with the community.

2020

Navigated the pandemic by pivoting to contactless takeout and delivery. The community's support kept the doors open. Launched on Uber Eats and DoorDash.

2023

Celebrated 19 years in business. Achieved a 4.6-star rating on Uber Eats with over 5,000 reviews. Still family-owned, still cooking from the heart.

Today

22+ years strong. The second generation of the Leng family now works alongside the first, carrying the tradition forward.
