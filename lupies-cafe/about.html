The Story of Monroe Road

Serving warm comfort food and friendly hospitality to Charlotte since 1987.

Founded in 1987

Lupie's Cafe was opened in 1987 by founder Lupie Duran on Monroe Road, in what was then a quiet stretch of East Charlotte. Her vision was simple: a neighborhood café that served satisfying comfort food at honest prices, where anyone could walk in and feel instantly at home.

From the beginning, the café became a community anchor, attracting locals, artists, students, and travelers alike with its cozy dining rooms and friendly staff.

The Living Room Cafe

Lupie's is famous for its homey, mismatched atmosphere. With vintage signs, license plates covering the walls, and mismatched dining tables, eating here feels like sitting in your best friend's living room.

We believe in slow-cooked meals and fast, warm service. Our dishes are served in simple pottery bowls, and our sweet iced tea is always full. It's an unpretentious refuge in a rapidly changing city, where local history and cozy food are preserved daily.

A Chili Legacy

Our four distinct chili styles were born out of a desire to serve everyone. Whether you want the intense heat of beanless Texas beef chili, the unique sweet-savory cinnamon notes of Cincinnati chili, the classic comfort of Southern mild chili, or a hearty organic vegetarian bean blend, Lupie's has a recipe crafted just for you.
