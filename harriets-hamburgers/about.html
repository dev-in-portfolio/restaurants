Our Story

From a Mother-in-Law's Name to Charlotte's Best Hamburger

The Beginning

In the fall of 2020, three friends with big dreams and a shared love of great food opened a smash burger stall at Optimist Hall in Charlotte, North Carolina. Brian Stockholm, founder of the beloved Papi Queso, teamed up with Joe Haubenhofer of The Plaid Penguin and Collin Ricks to create something special.

They named it after someone close to their hearts — Harriet, Brian's mother-in-law. It felt right. A family name for a place built on family, community, and really, really good burgers.

The Burgers

Harriet's uses Creekstone Farms Premium Black Angus beef — no hormones, no antibiotics. The patties are smashed thin on a searing hot griddle, creating that perfect crispy, caramelized crust. They're served on soft Parker House buns with house-made sauces that you won't find anywhere else.

The menu is simple by design: The OG, The Royale, and sides done right. But there's more than meets the eye — the Jucy Lucy, a Minnesota-style cheese-stuffed burger, lives on the secret menu. Ask for it by name.

Be Good

Two words hang on the wall at every Harriet's location: Be Good.

It's more than a mantra — it's how they run the business. The Be Good Crew program honors local heroes in the community. Staff, regulars, neighbors — anyone making Charlotte a better place gets recognized.

And then there's Henry's Monster Cookies. Henry is a young entrepreneur who bakes cookies and sells them through Harriet's, with all proceeds going to Halfway There Rescue, a local animal rescue organization. It's the kind of thing that makes a burger joint feel like home.

The Recognition

Charlotte took notice. Harriet's was voted Charlotte's Best Hamburger by the Charlotte Observer. On delivery platforms, the numbers speak for themselves — a 4.8 rating on DoorDash across 8,000+ orders and a 4.7 on Uber Eats with 3,000+.

What started as one stall at Optimist Hall has grown to five locations across the Charlotte area, from Plaza Midwood to Davidson. But the heart of it all stays the same: great burgers, real people, and the simple idea that food can bring happiness.

"Hamburgers & Happiness."

That's not just a tagline. That's the whole point.
