Our Story

Patience, Craft, Perseverance

From Inspiration to Bowl

Menya Daruma was born from a love of handmade noodles and the ritual of a perfect bowl of ramen. After years of studying under master ramen chefs in Tokyo and Fukuoka, our founder brought the traditions of Japan's menya to Charlotte's Elizabeth neighborhood.

The name Daruma comes from the traditional Japanese wishing doll inspired by Bodhidharma — a symbol of perseverance, good fortune, and resilience. Like the Daruma doll that always returns to its upright position, our kitchen pushes forward daily, refining and perfecting every element of the craft.

Every bowl begins with noodles made from scratch each morning, using Japanese flour and carefully timed rest periods. Our broths simmer for 18 hours, bones crackling and aromatics infusing until the stock reaches its full depth. We hand-make our chashu, marinate our ajitama, and build tare blends from ingredients sourced both locally and from Japan.

Our Philosophy

Ramen is not fast food — it is a craft that demands patience. At Menya Daruma, we believe in doing fewer things better. Our menu is focused and intentional. We do not cut corners on broth time, noodle texture, or tare composition. Every detail matters, from the temperature of the bowl to the cut of the scallion.

We are proud to serve Charlotte's Elizabeth neighborhood — a community that values authentic food made with care. Whether you sit at the counter to watch your bowl being assembled or grab an abura soba for a quick lunch, you are part of the Menya Daruma story.
