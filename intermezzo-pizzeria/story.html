Our Story

From a Serbian family kitchen to Charlotte's Plaza-Midwood

Intermezzo Pizzeria opened its doors in 2007 on East 10th Street, at the crossroads of Plaza-Midwood and Uptown Charlotte. What started as a family dream quickly became a neighborhood institution — a place where Serbian hospitality meets Italian tradition, and every meal feels like a gathering around the family table.

The restaurant was built on a simple philosophy: cook everything from scratch, use real ingredients, and never cut corners. That means handmade pizza dough stretched fresh every day, mozzarella made in-house, sauces that simmer for hours, and vegetables prepped each morning. There are no freezers in the kitchen. No microwaves. Just a stove, an oven, and a family's worth of recipes.

The menu reflects the owners' heritage — traditional Serbian family recipes passed down through generations, alongside beloved Italian classics. Karadjordje's schnitzel, cevapi, stuffed peppers, and eggplant musaka sit comfortably next to NY-style pizza, veggie lasagna, and pasta. It's a culinary crossover that makes Intermezzo genuinely unique in Charlotte.

The Neighborhood

Plaza-Midwood is one of Charlotte's most character-rich neighborhoods — a mix of historic bungalows, indie shops, street art, and a dining scene that prizes authenticity over trends. Intermezzo sits at the edge of it all, just minutes from Uptown, on a quiet stretch of East 10th Street that feels like a world away from the high-rises.

The dining room, with its stone and brick walls, warm lighting, and European café ambiance, invites lingering. In warmer months, the patio opens up with views of the Charlotte skyline — a favorite spot for pizza, cocktails, and conversation.
