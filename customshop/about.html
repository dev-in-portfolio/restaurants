Our Story

A small restaurant on Elizabeth Avenue that became a Charlotte institution.

From One Chef's Vision

In 2007, Chef Trey Wilson opened Customshop Handcrafted Food on Elizabeth Avenue. The concept was simple: cook with seasonal ingredients, make the pasta by hand, and build a restaurant that felt like part of the neighborhood.

The restaurant survived the Gold Line trolley construction that disrupted Elizabeth Avenue for months. It survived a pandemic. Through it all, the staff—most of whom had been there a decade or more—kept the kitchen running and the neighborhood fed.

A New Chapter

In summer 2022, Chef Andres Kaifer acquired Customshop. He kept the name, the concept, and every member of the staff. A Miami-born Cuban American and graduate of the Culinary Institute of America, Kaifer brought experience from The Durham Hotel, Vidrio, and Mac's Hospitality Group. He also brought a commitment to mentorship that has defined the restaurant's culture.

Today, roughly half the staff is Hispanic. Kaifer devotes 10 to 15 hours each week to informal mentoring. The kitchen hosts pop-ups from up-and-coming cooks. The menu changes weekly. The flan is the same recipe it's always been.
