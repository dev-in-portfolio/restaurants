Our Story

Ever Andalo is named after Andalo, a small mountain village in Italy where owner Jeff Tonidandel's family originates. That connection to place — the warmth, the food, the sense of community — is at the heart of everything we do.

We're a cozy neighborhood trattoria in Charlotte's NoDa district, the former textile mill area that's now one of the city's most vibrant neighborhoods for food, art, and culture. Our space is warm, colorful, and welcoming — the kind of place where you settle in and stay awhile.

Our kitchen serves Italian food made with care: homemade pasta rolled fresh daily, burrata made in-house, and seasonal dishes rooted in Italian tradition. We were honored to be recognized in the MICHELIN Guide — a testament to our team's dedication to doing things the right way.

Wine Program

Our all-Italian wine list is curated by sommeliers Michael Klinger (Level III certified) and Catherine Dawson (Level I certified). Every bottle on the list comes from Italy — from the bold reds of Piedmont and Tuscany to the crisp whites of Sicily and the Amalfi Coast.

Cocktails

Our cocktail program is led by Mixologist Colleen Hughes, who brings Italian flavors and classic technique to every glass. From perfect Negronis to seasonal Italian-inspired originals, our bar is a destination in its own right.

Kitchen

Every day, our kitchen makes pasta from scratch. Burrata is formed fresh. Focaccia comes out of the oven warm. The menu changes with the seasons, but the commitment to making things by hand — the Italian way — never does.
