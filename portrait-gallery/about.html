The Gallery's History

A fusion of Matthews heritage, fine visual arts, and modern culinary design.

The Historic Canvas

The Portrait Gallery resides in a beautifully restored historic brick building located at 118 E. Charles Street, built in the early 20th century. Originally serving the local community as a depot warehouse space during the heights of Matthews' historic rail era, the building retains its original century-old exposed brick, rustic wood trusses, and high rafters.

We restored the building with a vision to preserve Matthews' historical soul while grafting a modern, sophisticated cocktail lounge aesthetic onto it—offering guests a cozy sanctuary where history feels alive.

Gastronomy as Art

We believe that a plate of food or a poured drink should be treated like a canvas. Our culinary team works hand-in-hand with local farmers and suppliers around Charlotte to secure organic, seasonal ingredients.

Our cocktail program is equally expressive. By combining custom-infused spirits, house-made botanical syrups, and dramatic presentation styles, we transform simple cocktails into liquid masterpieces that are as beautiful to look at as they are satisfying to sip.

Supporting Local Artists

In keeping with our name, our walls feature a curated exhibition of rotating portraits and abstract paintings created by talented local artists from Matthews, Charlotte, and the wider Carolinas. Every piece hanging on our walls is available for purchase, with 100% of the proceeds going directly to the artists themselves. By dining with us, you are actively supporting local creative culture.
