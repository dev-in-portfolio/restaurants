📚 Our Story

About Calle Sol

From Cuba to Peru, serving Latin flavors in Charlotte's neighborhoods

Calle Sol started with a simple idea: bring the bold, fresh flavors of Latin America to Charlotte's neighborhood dining scene. Not a single country's cuisine, but the full spectrum—from Cuban comfort food to Peruvian ceviche, from Caribbean heat to South American soul.

The Concept

"Calle Sol" means "Sun Street" in Spanish—and that's the energy we bring. Bright flavors, warm service, and a atmosphere that feels like a celebration every night. Our kitchen moves from Cuba to Peru and everywhere in between: Cuban fried rice, Peruvian chaufa, classic ceviche, and mojitos that taste like summer.

The Food

Our ceviche is made to order, never frozen. Our Cuban fried rice—the dish that put us on the map—combines roasted lechon, caramelized pineapple, and Sol sauce in a way no one else does. We source locally when we can, and every dish is built around fresh, bold ingredients.

The menu spans countries and cultures: lechon asado, vaca frita, chaufa, tallerin. Each dish tells a story, and we're happy to tell it to you.

The Team

Calle Sol is part of the FS Food Group family—the same team behind Midwood Smokehouse, Yafo Kitchen, and Paco's Tacos. We believe in neighborhood restaurants that serve real food to real people, and we bring that philosophy to every plate.

The Community

We're proud to be part of two Charlotte neighborhoods: Plaza Midwood's eclectic, walkable vibe and SouthPark's polished energy. Both locations welcome dogs on the patio, serve kids with a smile, and treat every guest like family.
