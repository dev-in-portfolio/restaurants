💖 A Family Legacy

Our Story

From Matanzas, Cuba to Charlotte, North Carolina. A father's dream that lives on.

The Name

"El Puro" is Cuban slang for "dad" or "papa." It is also the word for "cigar." For us, it means both.

The name is a tribute to our father, Idael Perez, who dreamed of bringing authentic Cuban cuisine to the Carolinas. In 2017, he opened Havana Carolina Restaurant & Bar in Concord, NC — a reflection of everything he loved about the food, music, and spirit of Cuba.

2015 — The Arrival

The Perez family emigrated from Matanzas, Cuba to North Carolina in 2015. Manny, his sister Ana, and their mother Dania carried with them the recipes, rhythms, and warmth of Cuban culture.

In 2017, their father Idael opened Havana Carolina. He poured everything into the restaurant — the flavors of his childhood, the music of his homeland, the hospitality that defines Cuban culture.

2021 — The Dream Continues

When Idael was tragically killed in a car accident in 2017, the family faced an impossible loss. But the dream didn't die with him. Manny and Ana carried their father's vision forward.

In November 2021, they opened El Puro Cuban Restaurant on South Boulevard in Charlotte's Madison Park neighborhood. Every detail — the gold chandeliers, the vintage Cuban photos, the centerpiece bar made from a Chevrolet body — is a tribute to the Havana their father remembered.

Executive Chef Carlos Nevarez brings the bold, honest flavors of Cuban cuisine to every plate. The family — Manny, Ana, and their mother Dania — welcomes you like one of their own.
