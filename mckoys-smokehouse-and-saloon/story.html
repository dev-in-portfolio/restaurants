Our Story

From family recipes to South Charlotte tradition — scratch-made BBQ since 2006.

McKoy's Smokehouse & Saloon began with a simple observation by brothers Ryan and Jim Register and their father Namon: the Charlotte area needed more scratch-made kitchens. Too many restaurants were cutting corners with pre-made ingredients and frozen foods. The Registers knew they could do better.

They gathered the family's best recipes — the ones that had been perfected over generations of Sunday dinners, backyard barbecues, and holiday feasts — and began planning. In 2006, that dream became reality when McKoy's Smokehouse & Saloon opened its doors on Old Pineville Road.

From day one, the philosophy was simple: scratch-made or nothing. Every sauce is crafted in-house. Every side dish starts from raw ingredients. Every piece of meat is smoked low and slow over hardwood until it reaches perfection. The Registers source the freshest locally sourced meats and vegetables, supporting local farms and ensuring every plate is as fresh as it is flavorful.

Over the years, McKoy's has grown from a family project into a South Charlotte tradition. The dining room has a warm, rustic saloon feel — adorned with beer signs, sports jerseys, and the kind of character that only comes from years of serving good food to good people. The covered deck, complete with heaters and misters, makes McKoy's a year-round destination.

Today, McKoy's is known for its pit-smoked pot roast, legendary smokehouse wings, creamy mac and cheese, and the kind of Southern hospitality that makes every guest feel like family. The Registers also give back to the community as proud supporters of Purple Heart Homes.

For nearly two decades, McKoy's has been the place Charlotte goes for real BBQ — made from scratch, smoked with care, and served with a smile.
