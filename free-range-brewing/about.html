Our Story

A nano brewery with a big mission.

The Beginning

Free Range Brewing opened in 2015 in Charlotte's NoDa neighborhood — a small nano brewery with a big idea. We wanted to prove that beer could be an expression of place. That what grows in the soil around a city could shape the flavors in your glass.

95% Locally Sourced

From the very start, we committed to sourcing 95% of our ingredients locally — from metro farms to distant farmland across the region. It's not a marketing claim. It's the foundation of everything we brew. Every hop, every grain, every adjunct tells a story of the land it came from.

Collaboration is Everything

We work hand-in-hand with local farmers, chefs, and artists. Our beers are named after local stories — Therapy Session, Cream Of The Crop, Jenny Bought A Farm — because every batch is rooted in community. We've built relationships that go beyond transactions. These are the people who make Charlotte what it is.

More Than a Brewery

Free Range isn't just a place to drink beer. It's a gathering place. Live music on the patio. Comedy shows on weeknights. Figure drawing sessions. Community meetings. Food trucks rolling up on weekends. We believe a brewery should be the living room of a neighborhood — a place where people connect, create, and belong.

The Bar at 301

We grew. Our second location, The Bar at 301 Camp Rd, gives us more room to do what we do — more taps, more events, more space for the community to gather. Same spirit, different neighborhood.
