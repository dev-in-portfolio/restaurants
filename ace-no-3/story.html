Our Story

How a simple idea — great burger, cold beer, no fuss — grew into four Charlotte locations.

ACE No. 3 started with a bet on simplicity. In an industry that keeps adding, we chose to subtract.

Charlotte restaurateurs Andrew Chapman and Paul Manley had spent years building seafood concepts — Sea Level NC, The Waterman Fish Bar, Packard Tavern. They knew the Charlotte dining scene inside out. But what they really wanted was something smaller. A counter-service burger joint that stripped everything back to what mattered: the burger.

The idea was almost stubborn in its restraint. A short menu. Freshly griddled patties. Toasted potato rolls from Golden Grains Bakery. Housemade sauces. Cold beer. No reservations, no servers, no pretense.

2019

The original opens in Belmont Village

Chapman and Manley remodeled a brick-and-mortar space in the Belmont neighborhood and opened the first ACE No. 3. The interior was kept intentionally clean and simple — pastel green blocking, cognac-colored stools, red brick, quality wood tops.

2020

Best Burger — Charlotte Magazine

ACE No. 3 won its first Best Burger award from Charlotte Magazine. The recognition put the tiny counter-service spot on the map. More awards would follow.

2021

Center City opens

ACE expanded to Uptown, taking a spot in the One South building at 101 S Tryon Street. The second location brought smash burgers to the lunch crowd and after-work beer seekers.

2022

Concord Mills & Myers Park

Two more locations opened, bringing ACE to four. Myers Park at 829 Providence Road and Concord Mills rounded out the roster. Burger Bucks loyalty program launched.

2026

Still going. Still simple.

Four locations. Four-time Best Burger winner. Same griddles, same philosophy. Less is more.

The Philosophy

We believe that less is more. Our menu is simple, but pretty darn delicious. Fresh ingredients, cooked to order, served without attitude. A burger, a shake, a beer. That’s all you need.

— Founders Andrew Chapman & Paul Manley

Part of Something Bigger

ACE No. 3 is a proud member of High Tide Hospitality Group, a Charlotte-based collection of independent restaurants. From seafood to tavern fare to smash burgers, each concept serves its own purpose — but the commitment to quality, hospitality and local identity runs through all of them.
