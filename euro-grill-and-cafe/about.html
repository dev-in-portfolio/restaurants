🌎 From Bosnia to Charlotte

Our Story

From Srebrenica to Central Avenue. A family's journey, a community's kitchen.

Srebrenica

In 1995, during the Bosnian War, Dino Mehic's father was killed in the Srebrenica massacre. Dino, his mother, and his siblings survived. Three years later, in 1998, the family came to the United States as refugees, settling in Charlotte's Central Avenue corridor—where Bosnian refugees were gathering to rebuild their lives.

Bosna Market

Amela worked warehouse jobs while Dino stayed home with their children, Mehmed and Minela. In 2003, they opened Bosna Market on Central Avenue to serve the Bosnian refugee community—importing meats, cheeses, sodas, and seasonings from Bosnia, Serbia, Croatia, and across Europe. The market became a gathering place, a taste of home for people who had traveled thousands of miles.

Euro Grill & Cafe

When Bosna Market moved to larger quarters at 2719 Central Avenue, Dino and Amela added a tiny cafe. They opened a doorway through the market and stepped down into a small dining room with just a handful of tables. Euro Grill & Cafe was born—organic, humble, and rooted in community.

Today, Dino runs the front of the house with warmth and humor. He'll recommend Turkish coffee, tell you about Bosnia, and gently chide you if you rush through your coffee. The food is authentic: cevaps grilled to order, lepinja bread baked fresh, burek made from family recipes. On busy weekends, family friends like chef Lole Jelkic—trained in Bosnia—help in the kitchen.
